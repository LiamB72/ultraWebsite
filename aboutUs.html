Levels Weapons About Us Game On Steam

How the project went and felt

Greetings machine! This website was made by two people: me aka Stormy and Liam aka Unknown, for a school project. This project, which is still yet unfinished as there will be more added in the future as we gather more coding experience, was fully inspired by our mutual love for the game Ultrakill on which we have spent copious amounts of time both in game and in the community for all the fire memes they have which you can see all throughout the page in more or less hidden areas including the favicon because why not? This was very fun to do (((even if we spent a lot of the winter holidays playing modded Terraria instead of actively working on this))) but the finished product is still something we are very proud of as we spent a lot of time thinking and tinkering with the various UIs to make something near and dear to our hearts. This can be seen especially in the levels and weapons page as they fully take from the actual in game menus, and even though we wanted to add more content like a "pop culture" one to discuss the memes and impact of the game on the internet through the music and the new standard of movement shooters, we didn't quite know how to approach this section as it would have been quite major to not skip over any important information.

Behind the scenes

I'll start with Liam here. He took care of the Javascript you see on the weapons page after we decided to completely remodel that page as it wasn't on par with what we wanted to do with the website, and he also did the header right at the start of the project as he is the one who started it and invited me in. He did, however, also do a good amount of backend work cleaning up my messy, but functional code, as we went along since he knows how to organize things whereas I don't really think about that at the moment, and for that I am very grateful.

As for me, Stormy, I came up with all the Levels page design as well as doing the backgrounds, resizing images, that you see in the act pages and the weapons tab, so that they all followed the same format which was quite the pain to do in GIMP but was necessary to keep the website looking consistent. I also wrote all the weapon descriptions myself as I felt using the wiki wasn't very fun, and I really wanted to input my own thoughts on the weapoons as my over 200 hours of playtime qualify me to discuss them properly. I also did some of the more niche things like the favicons where I had fun putting memes in (after asking Unknown first ofc). I learned a lot about web development, and I hope to carry on work on this website throughout my years at university.
