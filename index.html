Introduction to the game

ULTRAKILL's steam page description says this about it: "ULTRAKILL is a fast-paced ultraviolent retro FPS combining the skill-based style scoring from character action games with unadulterated carnage inspired by the best shooters of the '90s. Rip apart your foes with varied destructive weapons and shower in their blood to regain your health."
Its development was started by Arsi Patala, aka Hakita (see picture below) He published his first dev log on 01/02/2018 to his YouTube channel, though the game was only released in early access on steam the third of september 2020 so almost 3 years later through New Blood Interactive, a game publisher that mainly takes care of games similar to ULTRAKILL like Dusk.

He has stated it to be inspired by multiple old shooters like doom classic and arena shooters like quake, both games having had a huge impact on games as a whole but also being seen heavily in ULTRAKILL. All in the level design, the weapons, all reminiscent of the classic fps feel and even more noticeable in the graphics, with them being purposefully retro and low poly.
It is, however, important to note that while hakita started this alone as a passion project, the scope of it grew and there is now a small team of devs that work on the game with 2 main ones, Hakita whom we've just mentioned and PITR who, according to Hakita himself, is a much better coder than him. The game's premice is you play as a blood powered robot named V1 who just travelled into hell and is looking for more blood to fuel itself, this is seen during the introduction to the game with the key phrase: "MANKING IS DEAD, BLOOD IS FUEL, HELL IS FULL" (see image below).

To say the game is fast paced is a gross understatement as thanks to your extensive and symbiotic arsenal the game goes really, REALLY fast and doesn't hold your hand like a lot of modern games, dropping you straight into the action after a very brief introduction to game mechanics with the rest needing to be learned by the player through trial and error like railcoining and projectile boosting. This freedom leaves the player curious about what is possible as every weapon has been carefully made to have interactions with the other, as such there is always a way to do more and more damage and the learning curve is essentially infinite.
